Challenge

Fashion entrepreneurs struggle to find the right manufacturer. Reddit threads, blog posts, and interviews revealed repeated friction:

  • Overwhelming choices without clear differentiation
  • Lack of transparency in pricing and capabilities
  • Communication barriers with overseas suppliers
  • No centralized platform for discovery and comparison

Together, we set out to simplify this processâ€”building trust, clarity, and speed through AI-powered discovery.

My Role

As a Product Designer, I:

  • Led user research with 12+ fashion entrepreneurs to understand pain points
  • Designed the end-to-end user experience from search to supplier match
  • Created Pomu's brand identity and visual language from scratch
  • Built a scalable design system that reduced development time by 30%
  • Collaborated with engineers to implement AI-powered search features

Design Goals

From the start, I set two key objectives:

1. Simplify Complex Decisions

Make manufacturer discovery feel as easy as shopping online, with smart filters and visual search capabilities.

2. Build Trust Through Transparency

Surface verified information about capabilities, certifications, and past work to help founders make confident choices.

Process

1. User Discovery

  • Method: Interviews with 12+ early-stage fashion entrepreneurs
  • Tool: Typeform, Miro
  • Key Insight: Founders lacked the right vocabulary, not vision. We needed to support fuzzy inputs with strong visual cues.

2. Information Architecture

We mapped a guided search-to-match journey:

  • Smart onboarding to understand user needs
  • AI-powered search with image + text input
  • Curated results with transparent filtering
  • Direct communication tools with translation support

Why it mattered: Founders often abandon platforms when overwhelmedâ€”this structure kept confidence high and drop-offs low.

3. Designing the Experience

We designed a dual-input search experience. I focused on:

  • Visual hierarchy that guides without overwhelming
  • Progressive disclosure of complex information
  • Mobile-first responsive design for on-the-go founders
  • Accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1 AA compliance)

AI Search Experience

Users could upload an image of a fabric or product and add a few words describing what they needed. The AI would then return smart, relevant results based on both inputs.

My goal was to make this feel simple and intuitive:

  • Clean upload interface with drag-and-drop functionality
  • Real-time visual feedback during processing
  • Smart suggestions based on uploaded images
  • Clear result matching with confidence scores

This was especially helpful for small business owners who had a vision but didn't always know the technical terms. By combining image and text input, we made the search smarter, more flexibleâ€”and most importantly, more human.

Design System

Together, we built a responsive design system with:

  • Modular component library (buttons, cards, forms)
  • Consistent typography scale and spacing system
  • Accessible color palette with proper contrast ratios
  • Flexible grid system for various screen sizes

Outcome: We cut dev time by 30% and ensured a cohesive, future-proof interface.
